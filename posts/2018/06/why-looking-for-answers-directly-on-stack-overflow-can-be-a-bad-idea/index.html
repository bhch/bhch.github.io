Why looking for answers directly on Stack Overflow can be a bad idea

Wed 06 June 2018   ·   Programming   ·   thoughts rant

I love Stack Overflow. It has helped me a lot through the years. Saved me time and money as I have no formal education in computer science.

Alright, enough ass-kissing. Let me get to the point. If you ever find yourself stuck at something, your first instinct is to search on Google for the answer. And I'm sure the top results are from Stack Overflow.

But if you ever wanted to become a better programmer, skip this ritual of searching on Google for an answer. Go to the official documentation and read.

The documentations are divided into multiple chapters. Look for the topic you think your problem is related to and read.

Docs will make you a better programmer¶

Reading the docs and crafting a solution to a problem is more creative. It will force you to think for a solution.

Another advantage of this approach is while you're looking for a solution in the docs, you will pick up other great things along the way. It has happened to me a lot of times. The docs are full of priceless tips and gems and you'll only find them when you read them.

Looking for an answer straight on Stack Overflow will help you solve the problem, but it won't teach you anything new. You are getting a solution all cooked up. All you have to do is modify the code a little and you're done. You don't have to think about the code, you don't have to do jacksh*t. That won't make you a better programmer.

When is it okay to Stack Overflow¶

Once you've managed to come up with a solution strictly from the docs and your wits, it's time to go to Stack Overflow to see if there are better ways to solve your problem. If yes, take that solution. Now you know two ways of solving your problem. You also know what's good practice and what's not-so-good practice.

If the solutions on SO are long and slow and are just bad, post an answer with your solution.

Other cases when going to Stack Overflow directly is better than "wasting" your time reading the docs:

  1. If you're on a deadline.
  2. If you're working with a programming language or a framework on which you don't want to invest much time to learn it. Whenever I have to write JavaScript, I don't bother reading the docs.

Conclusion¶

If you're on a deadline, or don't really care, just Stack Overflow it.

If you want to be a better programmer, read them docs.

If you want to take it even further, read them source codes.
